# RoomieDanmark Monetization Strategy

## Core Principle

RoomieDanmark should monetize the moment around moving, not the desperation of needing a home.

The strongest brand promise is that users can search, list rooms, contact each other, and use SøgeAgent without a hidden paywall. That promise is especially valuable because the Danish room and rental market often charges users 300-400 kr. per month just to send messages.

Short term, revenue should come from clearly labeled sponsors and advertisers. Longer term, RoomieDanmark can add optional provider tools, partner revenue, and only much later carefully tested paywalls if the platform has enough liquidity to justify them.

## Phase 1: 0-6 Months

Goal: cover costs without hurting trust.

Keep free:

- Searching rooms
- Creating room listings
- Contacting users
- Creating SøgeAgent
- Basic profile and messaging

First revenue model: manual founding sponsors.

Recommended sponsor products:

- In-feed sponsored card on `/ledige-vaerelser`
- Native sponsor card on room detail pages
- Post-action sponsor after `Send besked` or `Opret annonce`
- Optional footer or landing-page partner strip later

Best first advertiser categories:

- Furniture and home: IKEA, JYSK, Sinnerup
- Insurance: indboforsikring providers such as Tryg, Alka, GF, Gjensidige
- Internet and telecom: Hiper, YouSee, Telia
- Moving and storage: flyttefirmaer, depotrum, storage
- Student banks and fintech: Lunar or banks with student products

Suggested launch pricing:

- Small test sponsor: 1.500-3.000 kr. per month
- Category sponsor: 5.000-10.000 kr. per month once traffic is meaningful
- Founding sponsor bundle: 3 months at a discounted fixed price in exchange for logo/testimonial permission

Recommended first offer:

> Bliv founding sponsor for Danmarks nye gratis roomie-platform. Jeres brand vises native og tydeligt markeret overfor unge, studerende og nyflyttere netop i det øjeblik, de skal indrette, forsikre og flytte ind.

## Phase 2: 6-12 Months

Goal: add revenue around move-in intent and provider convenience.

Partner revenue:

- Move-in checklist with sponsor or affiliate links
- Insurance signup partner
- Internet setup partner
- Furniture starter kit partner
- Moving/storage partner

Optional paid provider tools:

- Boost listing for 24 hours
- Highlighted listing card
- Extra exposure in newsletter or social posts
- Better applicant overview for providers
- Saved replies for providers with many messages

Optional trust features:

- Verified profile badge if verification has real cost or conversion value
- Safer contract or fremlejekontrakt generator
- Deposit safety partner only if legally and operationally solid

Avoid in this phase:

- Charging users to contact each other
- Charging users to see messages
- Seeker-side urgency mechanics that make desperate users feel punished

## Phase 3: 12-24 Months

Goal: monetize scale without breaking the brand.

Possible advanced models:

- Provider subscription for high-volume landlords or co-living operators
- B2B packages for campuses, kollegier, property managers, and co-living brands
- Advanced provider inbox: filters, tags, saved replies, applicant sorting, analytics
- Sponsored city/category hubs
- Optional premium seeker profile, if it stays non-essential

Hard paywalls should only be considered if:

- RoomieDanmark has strong liquidity, for example 10.000+ users
- There are enough active rooms that the product is clearly valuable
- Users have been warned clearly before any change
- FAQ, terms, landing copy, and onboarding are updated first
- The change is A/B tested or launched in a very limited scope

If hard paywalls are tested, provider-side paywalls are safer than seeker-side paywalls. Charging desperate room seekers is the easiest way to damage the mission.

## Recommended Build Order

1. Create data-driven sponsor inventory for native ads.
2. Launch in-feed sponsored card and room-detail sponsor block.
3. Create a one-page media kit.
4. Contact 20 relevant Danish brands manually.
5. Add a move-in checklist as the first sponsor/affiliate content page.
6. Add optional listing boosts after supply grows.
7. Add provider convenience tools when landlords have too many applicants.
8. Revisit paywalls only after strong marketplace liquidity.

## Media Kit Outline

Create one simple PDF or landing page with:

- Mission: RoomieDanmark makes room search free and fair
- Audience: young people, students, new movers, shared homes
- Timing: users are actively moving and buying setup products
- Placements: in-feed, room detail, post-action, move-in checklist
- Launch pricing: founding sponsor rates
- Trust promise: all sponsor placements are clearly labeled `Sponsoreret`
- Contact: `support@roomies.dk` or Julian directly

## Sponsor Placement Rules

Use these rules to protect trust:

- Always label sponsor content with `Sponsoreret`
- Max one sponsor unit per screen where practical
- Native design, but visually distinguishable from real room listings
- No gambling, payday loans, sketchy loan products, or aggressive credit offers
- No sensitive targeting based on gender, desperation, relationship status, religion, ethnicity, or similar signals
- No targeted ads to minors
- No dark patterns or confusing close/consent behavior

## Metrics To Track

Marketplace health:

- Active rooms
- Active seekers
- Message starts
- Reply rate
- Time to first reply
- Rooms marked rented

Sponsor value:

- Sponsor impressions
- Sponsor clicks
- Click-through rate
- Post-action sponsor clicks
- Move-in checklist clicks
- Partner conversions where available

Revenue readiness:

- Monthly active users
- Returning users
- Rooms per city
- Messages per listing
- Provider pain level: too many applicants, inbox overload, repeated questions

## What Not To Do Early

- Do not charge for contact in the first 0-6 months.
- Do not add aggressive interstitial ads.
- Do not hide normal listings under paid promoted listings.
- Do not sell user desperation as targeting.
- Do not make ads look like normal room listings without a clear sponsor label.
- Do not introduce hard paywalls while public copy still says everything is 100% free.

## Sources And Compliance Notes

- Native advertising should be transparent and clearly identifiable as advertising. See FTC guidance: https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/native-advertising-guide-businesses
- EU DSA guidance emphasizes ad transparency, clear ad labeling, protection of minors, and limits around sensitive data: https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/digital-services-act

This is a business strategy note, not legal advice. Before scaling ads, sponsor targeting, payment features, or verification products, review Danish/EU consumer, privacy, and advertising requirements.
